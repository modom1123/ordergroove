01 — The Alignment Problem

Roadmap clarity is the prerequisite

The hardest part of platform PM at a scaling company isn't the product decisions — it's getting Sales, CS, and Engineering to agree on which problems to solve first. When cross-functional alignment is uneven, you get roadmap churn, shifting priorities, and teams pulling in different directions.

At IMPLAN, I walked into exactly this: no shared platform, no common prioritization framework, competing stakeholder needs across the CEO's office, engineering, and internal operations. I built the operational backbone — a WSJF-prioritized roadmap informed by behavioral analytics and stakeholder input — that gave every team a shared frame of reference.

02 — Rules → Agents

Same PM problem.
Different execution layer.

Whether the triggering logic is a rules engine or an ML model, the product manager's ownership is identical: define the outcome, identify intervention points, design what success looks like, and measure impact. I've built the rule-based version. The Frontier roadmap is the evolution.

What I've built

Rule-Based Interventions

IMPLAN · Pendo + Behavioral Analytics
  • ◎
    Segment Define user cohorts by vertical, role, and behavior milestones
  • ⧫
    Trigger Set conditions: "when user in segment X completes action Y, fire guide Z"
  • ▸
    Act Deliver the predefined intervention — walkthrough, tooltip, prompt
  • ◉
    Measure Track outcome against baseline — 42% TTV reduction, 60% feature discovery lift
Where Frontier goes

Autonomous Agents

Ordergroove · Anticipate AI + Frontier
  • ◎
    Observe Ingest order cadence, consumption patterns, engagement signals in real-time
  • ⧫
    Reason Model determines this subscriber is at overstock risk — no predefined rule
  • ▸
    Act Agent selects the intervention: skip prompt, swap suggestion, frequency adjustment
  • ◉
    Learn Outcomes feed back into the model — the system improves without new PM rules

The bridge: In both models, the PM defines why the intervention exists and how we know it worked. The difference is whether I configure the trigger logic or partner with ML engineering to train the model that decides. The product thinking — outcome definition, user journey mapping, success metrics, iteration loops — is the same muscle. The Frontier roadmap is the evolution I'm ready to drive.
